# Reinforcement — Day 01

Today I worked on reinforcement to strengthen my logic in C.

The goal was not to learn new things, but to become better with what I already know.

I focused on:
- using while loops
- improving nested loops
- working with arrays
- translating logic into code faster

---

## Why I do reinforcement

I realized that understanding something once is not enough.

I need to repeat it, practice it, and apply it in different ways until it becomes natural.

Reinforcement helps me:
- become faster
- reduce mistakes
- think more clearly
- gain confidence in my code

---

## How I approach it

Instead of jumping to advanced topics, I take simple concepts and push them further.

I reuse the same logic:
- compare values
- count occurrences
- detect patterns

But in different exercises.

This helps me build a strong base.

---

## My strategy

Morning:
I learn something new.

Afternoon:
I do reinforcement.

I redo concepts with variations and harder exercises.

I increase the difficulty step by step (crescendo).

---

## What I am building

I am not just writing code.

I am building:
- a way of thinking
- a problem-solving method
- reflexes

---

## Important realization

If my basics are strong, everything later (pointers, advanced problems) will be easier.

So I take my time to master the fundamentals.

---

## My goal

I want to reach a level where:

I see a problem → and I immediately know how to solve it.

No hesitation.
Only logic.
